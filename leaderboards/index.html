Important! Read First!

These numbers are likely significant underestimates of the true player counts on games and can't easily be extrapolated to gather Stadia Player or Pro Subscriber counts or compared against eachother.

1. Some of the numbers may only show people who have manually (there are no defaults) set their privacy settings to allow: "Any Player" to see game activity.

This is definitely the case for early Thumper numbers, and probably more (e.g. early Zombie Army 4 numbers and possibly early players in other games).

2. In OMD3, all leaderborads were reset 9 days after launch. This unfortunately was the most active time for the game. Leaderboard sizes are probably underestimated by between 15K and 75K.

3. In multiple games, player counts are split across leaderboards in multiple difficulty settings. OMD3 and Hitman fall into this category. I tried to capture leaderboards for multiple difficulty levels in the spreadsheet where possible (though the plot only shows one) but can't in the case of Hitman due to a bug.

4. The amount of effort it takes to be shown on a leaderboard varies a lot from game to game (e.g. very little time in Monopoly to hours in Hitman).

5. Leaderboards for some games only contain data from online multiplayer sessions (e.g. Uno, Breakpoint)

6. Family share was released only on November 5, which likely hid mutliple users playing in one household.

7. Many Pro-subscribers either don't claim or don't play every Pro game because a) The genre isn't their Jam; b) They played the game already somewhere else; c) They have backlog of games to play (this is my case for most of the Pro games!); d) They have their own reasons... Don't be the guy who tries to extrapolate the total number of Stadia Pro subscribers from these numbers. They show what they show!
